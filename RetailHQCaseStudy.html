Effective lighting in retail stores plays a crucial role in attracting shoppers to specific departments and creating a pleasant shopping experience. However, many stores currently lack the necessary options to manage lighting experiences throughout their premises. The centralized control over creating unified lighting experiences across multiple stores is either prohibitively expensive or presents logistical challenges that make it cumbersome and time-consuming for headquarters to implement.

 

This limitation hinders stores from optimizing their lighting strategies based on different hours of the day and specific shopper preferences. As a result, they miss out on the opportunity to enhance customer satisfaction and create a more enjoyable shopping environment.

 

Recognizing this issue, there is a need for a solution that empowers stores to efficiently control and customize their lighting experiences. This solution should be cost-effective, easy to implement, and enable headquarters to effortlessly manage and coordinate lighting settings across all stores. By addressing these challenges, retailers can enhance the overall shopping experience, attract more customers, and ultimately drive business success.

Why did we choose Design Thinking?

 

The Retail industry presented us with a multitude of potential opportunities. However, we recognized the importance of not getting caught up in building solutions without a clear understanding of the most pressing problem faced by our users. To ensure we stayed focused and effective, we adopted a Design Thinking approach.

 

By bringing together individuals from diverse disciplines, we aimed to create a collaborative environment where we could delve deep into the needs of the Formula Manager and Facility Manager. Our objective was to gain a shared understanding of the challenges and goals they faced, enabling us to uncover the most critical problem to address. This approach allowed us to align our efforts towards developing meaningful and impactful solutions that directly addressed the real needs of our users.

CONCLUSION + LEARNINGS
What I’d do differently next time.

This project initially faced numerous uncertainties and relied heavily on assumptions. The teams lacked clarity on their deliverables and were unsure of what to expect. To address this, we conducted a Design Thinking workshop that provided valuable insights into the user journey and identified pain points. This workshop played a pivotal role in keeping our users at the center of the project and guiding our decisions.

 

  • Better navigation exploration - One significant challenge we encountered was the exploration of navigation options. The project had multiple versions with inconsistent navigation structures due to different development teams establishing their own approaches based on uncovered value propositions during the workshop. In the future, I would prioritize upfront planning to assess the scalability of the project and ensure a cohesive navigation structure, particularly for User Management workflows.
  • Better visuals - Another area for improvement is in the visual aspects of the design. While I had some limitations due to Interact guidelines, I believe I could have pushed myself to explore more aesthetic and intuitive visual solutions. Additionally, adhering to WCAG standards would have been a valuable consideration for accessibility.
  • Validate the end results - One aspect that could have been enhanced is the validation of the end results. Due to time constraints, I was unable to involve users in validating the outcomes derived from the workshop. In future projects, I would proactively ask the right questions to project managers to better forecast and allocate time for user validation.

 

Reflecting on the experience, I thoroughly enjoyed working with a diverse team spread across different locations. It provided me with opportunities to explore my capabilities as a User Advocate and as an individual. Building connections and friendships was an added bonus. I made a conscious effort to remain unbiased in my design decisions and deliver solutions that added value to the lives of our users.

 

Knowing that our work potentially contributes to someone's reduced stress and allows them to spend more time with their family brings a sense of fulfillment :)
