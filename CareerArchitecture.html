Career Architecture

During my tenure as Senior Manager UX at Target, I took on the responsibility of developing a comprehensive Career Architecture framework. This initiative aimed to provide individual designers with clarity and direction, establish goals and development skills, manage talent effectively, ensure performance management, and enhance employee engagement and retention.

 

In my role as Senior Manager UX, my responsibilities in this work included:

 

  • Creating a Career Architecture guide specifically tailored for individual designers. This guide aimed to offer them a clear understanding of their growth potential within the company, outlining key milestones to achieve at different experience levels.
  • Supporting the business by identifying high-potential employees and formulating succession plans for critical roles. By mapping out well-defined career paths and providing ample growth opportunities, we aimed to attract and retain talented individuals. Additionally, aligning employees' aspirations with the organization's long-term objectives was a crucial aspect.

 

To accomplish these objectives, I followed a structured process:

 

  • Identifying the current conditions and understanding the existing career landscape for designers within the organization.
  • Setting clear goals for the Career Architecture initiative, aligning them with the company's overall talent strategy.
  • Developing a well-thought-out strategy to create a flexible Career Architecture framework that accommodates the unique needs of individual designers while aligning with the organization's objectives.
  • Building the Career Architecture framework , ensuring it is adaptable and customizable to fit Target's specific requirements.
  • Collaborating with relevant stakeholders to refine and adapt the Career Architecture framework to the organization's context, ensuring its successful implementation.

 

Looking ahead, the next steps involve:

 

  • Communicating and disseminating the Career Architecture framework to the design team and relevant stakeholders, ensuring everyone understands its benefits and how to leverage it effectively.
  • Providing necessary training and resources to support designers in implementing and utilizing the Career Architecture framework in their professional development journey.

 

By undertaking these efforts, we aimed to empower designers, promote their growth and development, enhance talent management and performance evaluation processes, and ultimately foster a culture of engagement and retention within the organization.

Scalability of the framework

The next steps in the project are structured to be executed in phases, encompassing several key components:

 

  • Establishing Guidelines for KPIs and Performance Management: This phase focuses on developing clear guidelines for setting Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and implementing effective performance management practices. The objective is to provide flexible growth opportunities for designers while also assisting the business in fostering long-term employee retention.
  • Designer Engagement and Retention Plans: This phase involves the creation and implementation of comprehensive plans aimed at engaging designers through multiple opportunities. The objective is to cultivate a supportive and motivating work environment that encourages creativity, collaboration, and professional development, ultimately leading to higher retention rates.
  • Adapting the Career Architecture Framework to Different Roles: This phase focuses on refining and tailoring the Career Architecture Framework to suit the unique requirements of various roles within the organization. This adaptability ensures that the framework remains relevant and applicable across different job positions, allowing for consistent career development and growth opportunities.
  • Quick Start Guidelines for Easier Onboarding: This phase entails developing streamlined and user-friendly guidelines to facilitate the smooth onboarding of new designers. These guidelines will provide clear instructions and resources to help new hires understand and navigate the Career Architecture Framework efficiently, enabling them to kick-start their professional journeys effectively.

 

By executing these phases in a systematic manner, the project aims to establish robust performance management practices, enhance designer engagement and retention, ensure the Career Architecture Framework's versatility, and facilitate a seamless onboarding experience for new designers.

The career trajectories in UX often diverge from traditional linear paths, as there is no singular prescribed route to a successful career in this field.

Many existing career frameworks are designed around the notion of a linear path, which can limit individuals' ability to envision their own authentic career journeys.

 

To address this, we propose adopting the concept of a trellis as a metaphor for growth, care, and nurturing—the very qualities that resonate with human aspirations.

 

In the context of UX, the trellis represents the expansion and development of expertise across various practices, the acquisition of new skills, and the continuous growth in existing skills. It emphasizes the importance of holistic professional development and the ability to flourish in multiple dimensions within the field of UX.

20 shared skills across all UX disciplines

  • Creates the foundation for the trellis and empowers individuals to develop skillsets for their desired career regardless of discipline
  • Limit to ≤ 20 skills to manage complexity and maintain ability to scale
  • Skills are not roles (eg: UX Writing is a skill, Content Designer is a role)
  • Skills will differ by organization due to maturity, growth intention, etc
  • Skills should be concrete skills that can be developed and not squishy such as mindsets or behaviors (this will be addressed in a new way)
  • Skills may evolve with the maturity of the organization
  • Skills are not behaviors or mindsets
  • Accounts for both ICs and Leaders of People

Final documentation would include definitions for all skills

Aligned to Target’s talent philosophy: Knowledge, Impact, Communication

A Practice is a distinct job family in our UX organization

  • Each practice is distinct due to their core skills – the work performed in the practice is distinct
  • Practices are the recognized functions within our organization (shows up in Workday)

A Role is the job an individual is hired to do

  • Each practice may contain multiple roles
  • While roles within a practice share the same core skills, they are distinct by the distinguishing (secondary) skills
  • eg: within the practice of Product Design, we hire a variety of "types" of product designers – these are the roles while Product Design is the practice

Defining Practices and Roles

  • We should consider the full team of today as well as the future of each practice within it – how do we set up the architecture for today and for tomorrow? what roles do we not have today but want to build towards?
  • Within each practice and role, the levels for all skills will be considered
  • It's easy to consider the people in today's team; but when we create the framework agnostic of the people, this will help us to consider an evaluation process more objectively
