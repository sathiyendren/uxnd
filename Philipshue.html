The Philips Hue product line offers a variety of options designed to enhance the lighting experience in people's homes. The goal is to enable users to customize their lighting by combining bulbs in a single room, inspiring them to create unique and captivating lighting effects. One of the standout features of Philips Hue is the ability to combine the light from multiple bulbs, resulting in stunning gradient-like effects that go beyond traditional lighting systems. These combinations are referred to as "Scenes."

 

However, the current usage of the product by the majority of existing customers does not align with this intended use. According to a recent survey, only 46% of users reported using the scene feature on a regular basis, and a mere 23% mentioned actively creating and utilizing their own scenes.

I conducted remote interviews with 8 active users to delve into their experiences with the scenes feature and understand how they are currently utilizing it. The main objective was to explore their needs, pain points, and overall satisfaction with the existing user interface (UI). The interviews lasted approximately 30 minutes each and were aimed at uncovering potential opportunities for improvement and gathering insights on user satisfaction levels.

During the interviews, detailed notes were taken, capturing actual quotes, needs, wishes, and pain points expressed by the participants. These notes served as valuable data for analysis. After conducting all the interviews, the collected notes were organized into clusters based on similarities in statements, needs, wishes, and pain points. This clustering approach helped identify common themes and patterns.

 

From these clusters, key questions were formulated using the "How might we" (HMW) framework. This approach simplified the problem-solving process by focusing on specific questions rather than broad topics. The HMW questions were carefully evaluated to determine their importance and relevance to the overall objective.

Following the evaluation, idea sketches were created to explore potential solutions for the most critical HMW questions. These sketches provided a visual representation of possible design directions and served as a starting point for further exploration.

 

To prioritize features and focus on the most impactful areas, a value/effort matrix was employed. This matrix enabled the team to assess the value of each feature or idea in relation to the effort required for implementation. By considering both factors, the team could make informed decisions about which features to prioritize and allocate resources accordingly.

Personas and profiles were developed based on assumptions since there was a lack of actual qualitative and quantitative user research. In a team session, all the assumptions were gathered and used to create personas in a quick and informal manner. This approach ensured that everyone's opinions and perspectives were taken into account and formalized in the persona development process. While these personas may not be based on concrete user data, they served as a starting point for understanding user needs and guiding the design process.
